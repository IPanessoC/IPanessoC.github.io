Post: 16/03/26

As part of my foreign language training exercises, I decided to write a chronicle about a historical figure who
genuinely interests me. Long before Linux became the invisible digital backbone, Linus Torvalds was a curious
student in Finland. His early analytical drive led him to modify rudimentary operating systems. By 1991,
frustrated by existing limitations, he began writing his own educational system from scratch.
What he humbly called "just a hobby" ultimately disrupted the entire tech industry.
By releasing the Linux kernel under a free license, he invited global collaboration and built a stable foundation
for supercomputers and mobile devices. Later, he engineered Git to manage distributed development,
revolutionizing how software is built worldwide.
